Federated ADMM-IRLS Formulation

Suppose there are $m$ data centers. Center $i$ holds a local dataset $D_i=\{(x_{ij},y_{ij})\}_{j=1}^{n_i}$ for $i=1,\ldots,m$, where $x_{ij}\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ is a $p$-dimensional predictor vector and $y_{ij}\in\{0,1\}$ is a binary response for subject $j$ at center $i$. Let $X_i\in\mathbb{R}^{n_i\times p}$ denote the local design matrix whose $j^{\mathrm{th}}$ row is $x_{ij}^{\top}$, and $y_i=(y_{i1},\ldots,y_{in_i})^{\top}\in\{0,1\}^{n_i}$ denote the response vector. The goal is to estimate a single global sparse coefficient vector $\beta\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ that captures the relationship between predictors and response across all centers while preserving privacy.

For observation $j$ at center $i$, the LR model is

$$ \Pr(y_{ij}=1\mid x_{ij};\beta) =p_{ij}(\beta) =\frac{1}{1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta)}. $$

Because $y_{ij}$ follows a Bernoulli distribution with parameter $p_{ij}(\beta)$, its probability mass function is

$$ \Pr(y_{ij}\mid p_{ij}(\beta)) =p_{ij}(\beta)^{y_{ij}}\{1-p_{ij}(\beta)\}^{1-y_{ij}}. $$

Assuming conditional independence given $\beta$, the likelihood for center $i$ is

$$ L_i(\beta)=\prod_{j=1}^{n_i} p_{ij}(\beta)^{y_{ij}}\{1-p_{ij}(\beta)\}^{1-y_{ij}}. $$

Taking logarithms gives the log-likelihood

$$ \ell_i(\beta)=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left[y_{ij}\log p_{ij}(\beta)+(1-y_{ij})\log(1-p_{ij}(\beta))\right]. \tag{2.1} $$

The negative log-likelihood $\mathcal{L}_i(\beta)=-\ell_i(\beta)$ is minimized. Using the identities below,

$$ \log p_{ij}(\beta) =x_{ij}^{\top}\beta-\log\{1+\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta)\}, \qquad \log\{1-p_{ij}(\beta)\} =-\log\{1+\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta)\}, $$

By substituting into (2.1), the standard logistic loss is obtained

$$ \mathcal{L}_i(\beta) =\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left[\log\{1+\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta)\}-y_{ij}x_{ij}^{\top}\beta\right]. $$

To perform sparse estimation and variable selection, for a given center $i$, the $\ell_1$-penalized LR objective is

$$ \min_{\beta\in\mathbb{R}^{p}} \mathcal{L}_i(\beta)+\lambda\lVert\beta\rVert_1, \qquad \lambda>0. \tag{2.2} $$

To enable distributed optimization, a consensus formulation is adopted by introducing a local parameter $\beta_i\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ and a global consensus parameter $z\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$, yielding

$$ \begin{aligned} \min_{\{\beta_i\},z}\quad & \sum_{i=1}^{m}\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i)+\lambda\lVert z\rVert_1\\ \text{s.t.}\quad &\beta_i=z,\qquad i=1,\ldots,m. \end{aligned} $$

Following the scaled ADMM formulation, the resulting scaled augmented Lagrangian with dual variables $u_i$ and penalty parameter $\rho>0$ is

$$ \mathcal{L}_{\rho}(\{\beta_i\},z,\{u_i\}) =\sum_{i=1}^{m} \left[\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i)+\frac{\rho}{2}\lVert\beta_i-z+u_i\rVert_2^2\right] +\lambda\lVert z\rVert_1. \tag{2.3} $$

At ADMM iteration $k$, the local update at center $i$ solves the $\beta_i$-subproblem

$$ \beta_i^{k+1} =\arg\min_{\beta_i\in\mathbb{R}^{p}} \left\{\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i) +\frac{\rho}{2}\lVert\beta_i-z^k+u_i^k\rVert_2^2\right\}. $$

Local IRLS Update

Let

$$ \Phi_i^k(\beta_i) =\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i) +\frac{\rho}{2}\lVert\beta_i-z^k+u_i^k\rVert_2^2. $$

Then the local objective can be written as

$$ \beta_i^{k+1}=\arg\min_{\beta_i\in\mathbb{R}^{p}}\Phi_i^k(\beta_i). \tag{2.4} $$

Unlike linear regression, the logistic loss $\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i)$ is nonlinear, and the minimizer $\Phi_i^k(\beta_i)$ generally has no closed-form solution. $\beta_i^{k+1}$ is therefore computed by applying the Newton-Raphson method; for LR this corresponds to IRLS. Let $\beta_i^{(t)}$ denote the current inner IRLS iterate within the $k^{\mathrm{th}}$ ADMM iteration. Newton's update is

$$ \beta_i^{(t+1)} =\beta_i^{(t)}- \left[\nabla^2\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right)\right]^{-1} \nabla\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right). \tag{2.5} $$

To derive the gradient and Hessian, the fitted probabilities at the current inner iterate are defined as follows:

$$ p_{ij}^{(t)} =\Pr\!\left(y_{ij}=1\mid x_{ij};\beta_i^{(t)}\right) =\frac{1}{1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})}, \qquad p_i^{(t)}=\left(p_{i1}^{(t)},\ldots,p_{in_i}^{(t)}\right)^{\top}. $$

Gradient derivation

By differentiating the logistic loss and the quadratic ADMM penalty term with respect to $\beta_i^{(t)}$, the following expression is obtained:

$$ \begin{aligned} \nabla\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) &=\nabla\mathcal{L}_i\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) +\nabla\frac{\rho}{2}\left\lVert\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right\rVert_2^2\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \left[\log\{1+\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\} -y_{ij}x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)}\right] +\nabla\frac{\rho}{2}\left\lVert\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right\rVert_2^2\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left[ \frac{\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\,x_{ij}} {1+\exp(x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})} -y_{ij}x_{ij} \right] +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right)\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left[ \frac{1}{1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})} -y_{ij} \right]x_{ij} +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right)\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left(p_{ij}^{(t)}-y_{ij}\right)x_{ij} +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right). \end{aligned} $$

The gradient can be written compactly as

$$ \nabla\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) =X_i^{\top}\left(p_i^{(t)}-y_i\right) +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right). \tag{2.6} $$

Hessian derivation

To apply the IRLS update, the Hessian is also required. By differentiating $\nabla\Phi_i^k(\beta_i^{(t)})$ and evaluating at $\beta_i^{(t)}$, the following expression is obtained:

$$ \begin{aligned} \nabla^2\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) &=\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \nabla\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right)\\ &=\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \left[ \sum_{j=1}^{n_i}\left(p_{ij}^{(t)}-y_{ij}\right)x_{ij} +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right) \right]\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i}p_{ij}^{(t)}\right)x_{ij}^{\top} +\rho I_p\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \left(1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\right)^{-1} \right)x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left( -\left(1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\right)^{-2} \frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)}) \right)x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left( -\left(1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\right)^{-2} \exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)}) \frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i}(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)}) \right)x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \left( -\left(1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\right)^{-2} \exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})(-x_{ij}) \right)x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p\\ &=\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \frac{\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})} {\left(1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})\right)^2} x_{ij}x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p. \end{aligned} $$

Using the identities

$$ p_{ij}^{(t)} =\frac{1}{1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})}, \qquad 1-p_{ij}^{(t)} =\frac{\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})} {1+\exp(-x_{ij}^{\top}\beta_i^{(t)})}, $$

the Hessian is given by

$$ \nabla^2\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) =\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} p_{ij}^{(t)}\left(1-p_{ij}^{(t)}\right) x_{ij}x_{ij}^{\top}+\rho I_p. $$

The diagonal weight matrix is defined as

$$ W_i^{(t)} =\operatorname{diag}\!\left( p_{i1}^{(t)}\left(1-p_{i1}^{(t)}\right),\ldots, p_{in_i}^{(t)}\left(1-p_{in_i}^{(t)}\right) \right). $$

Therefore, the Hessian can be written compactly as

$$ \nabla^2\Phi_i^k\!\left(\beta_i^{(t)}\right) =\sum_{j=1}^{n_i} p_{ij}^{(t)}\left(1-p_{ij}^{(t)}\right)x_{ij}x_{ij}^{\top} +\rho I_p =X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p. \tag{2.7} $$

Newton-IRLS step

By substituting (2.6) and (2.7) into Newton's update (2.5), the local IRLS step used to compute $\beta_i^{(k+1)}$ is obtained:

$$ \beta_i^{(t+1)} =\beta_i^{(t)}- \left[X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right]^{-1} \left[ X_i^{\top}\left(p_i^{(t)}-y_i\right) +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right) \right]. \tag{2.8} $$

For implementation, explicit inversion is avoided, and (2.8) is expressed as a linear system. Both sides are multiplied by $[X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p]$ and rearrange:

$$ \begin{aligned} \left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right)\beta_i^{(t+1)} &=\left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right)\beta_i^{(t)}\\ &\quad- \left[ X_i^{\top}\left(p_i^{(t)}-y_i\right) +\rho\left(\beta_i^{(t)}-z^k+u_i^k\right) \right]. \end{aligned} $$

By expanding the right-hand side and canceling the $\rho\beta_i^{(t)}$ terms, the following expression is obtained:

$$ \left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right)\beta_i^{(t+1)} =X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i\beta_i^{(t)} -X_i^{\top}\left(p_i^{(t)}-y_i\right) +\rho\left(z^k-u_i^k\right). \tag{2.9} $$

To express (2.9) in the standard IRLS form, the working response is defined as

$$ z_{\mathrm{work}}^{(t)} =X_i\beta_i^{(t)}+ \left(W_i^{(t)}\right)^{-1}\left(y_i-p_i^{(t)}\right). $$

Then

$$ \begin{aligned} X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}z_{\mathrm{work}}^{(t)} &=X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)} \left[ X_i\beta_i^{(t)}+ \left(W_i^{(t)}\right)^{-1}\left(y_i-p_i^{(t)}\right) \right]\\ &=X_i^{\top} \left[ W_i^{(t)}X_i\beta_i^{(t)}+ \left(y_i-p_i^{(t)}\right) \right]\\ &=X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i\beta_i^{(t)} -X_i^{\top}\left(p_i^{(t)}-y_i\right). \end{aligned} $$

By substituting this identity into (2.9), the IRLS local update is obtained as the linear system. Thus, the local update equation in terms of the ADMM variables is

$$ \beta_i^{(t+1)} =\left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right)^{-1} \left( X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}z_{\mathrm{work}}^{(t)} +\rho\left(z^k-u_i^k\right) \right). \tag{2.10} $$

The above IRLS update is repeated at each center until convergence of the local objective $\Phi_i^k(\beta_i)$, and the converged iterate is taken as the ADMM local update, i.e.,

$$ \beta_i^{k+1}=\beta_i^{(t_{\mathrm{final}})}. $$

Global Consensus and Dual Updates

After all centers compute their local parameters $\beta_i^{k+1}$, the global consensus parameter $z$ is next updated. Following the scaled ADMM framework, this step is obtained by minimizing the scaled augmented Lagrangian in (2.3) with respect to $z$, while $\{\beta_i^{k+1}\}_{i=1}^{m}$ and $\{u_i^k\}_{i=1}^{m}$ are treated as fixed. Therefore, the $z$-subproblem is

$$ z^{k+1}=\arg\min_z \left\{ \lambda\lVert z\rVert_1 +\frac{\rho}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{m} \lVert\beta_i^{k+1}-z+u_i^k\rVert_2^2 \right\}. \tag{2.11} $$

Define

$$ b_i^{k+1}=\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k, \qquad \bar b^{k+1}=\frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}b_i^{k+1}. $$

The quadratic term in (2.11) can be written as

$$ \sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert z-b_i^{k+1}\rVert_2^2, $$

and using the standard identity

$$ \sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert z-b_i^{k+1}\rVert_2^2 =m\lVert z-\bar b^{k+1}\rVert_2^2 +\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert b_i^{k+1}-\bar b^{k+1}\rVert_2^2, $$

where the second term is constant with respect to $z$. After this constant is dropped, the $z$-subproblem (2.11) is reduced to

$$ z^{k+1}=\arg\min_z \left\{ \lambda\lVert z\rVert_1 +\frac{m\rho}{2}\lVert z-\bar b^{k+1}\rVert_2^2 \right\}. \tag{2.12} $$

This $z$-subproblem (2.12) is separable across coordinates and has a closed-form solution via componentwise soft-thresholding. $S_{\kappa}(\cdot)$ denotes the soft-thresholding operator with threshold $\kappa>0$, defined for $t\in\mathbb{R}$ by

$$ S_{\kappa}(t)=\operatorname{sign}(t)\max\{|t|-\kappa,0\}, $$

and applied elementwise to vectors. With $\kappa=\lambda/(m\rho)$, the global aggregation step is given by

$$ z^{k+1} =S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}\!\left(\bar b^{k+1}\right) =S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}\!\left( \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}\left(\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k\right) \right). $$

After updating the global consensus parameter $z^{k+1}$, the scaled dual variables are updated locally at each center according to

$$ u_i^{k+1}=u_i^k+\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}, \qquad i=1,\ldots,m. $$

Through this dual update, the current consensus mismatch $\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}$ is accumulated; as the iterates approach agreement across centers, the mismatch is reduced and the dual variables are stabilized. The iterates $\{(\beta_i^k,z^k,u_i^k)\}$ are generated by successively performing the local $\beta_i$-update, the global $z$-update, and the dual update until convergence, which is assessed using the primal and dual residual criteria described next.

Convergence Diagnostics

The ADMM iterations are converged when both the primal and dual residuals meet pre-specified convergence conditions. The scaled ADMM form with penalty parameter $\rho>0$ is adopted. The primal residual measures the degree of disagreement (consensus violation) across the $m$ sites and is defined as

$$ \lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2 =\left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}. $$

The dual residual measures the change in the consensus variable between successive iterations and is given by

$$ \lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2 =\rho\sqrt{m}\,\lVert z^{k+1}-z^k\rVert_2. $$

Following standard practice, stopping tolerances are defined using absolute and relative terms, $\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}},\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}>0$. The primal tolerance is

$$ \epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}} =\sqrt{mp}\,\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}} +\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}} \max\left\{ \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert\beta_i^{k+1}\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}, \sqrt{m}\,\lVert z^{k+1}\rVert_2 \right\}, $$

while the dual tolerance is

$$ \epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}} =\sqrt{mp}\,\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}} +\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}\rho \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert u_i^k\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}. $$
The algorithm is terminated when both $\lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}}$ and $\lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}}$. In this study, $\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}}=10^{-5}$ and $\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}=10^{-4}$ are used, and a maximum number of iterations is imposed as a safeguard.

Algorithm: Federated ADMM-IRLS

The complete federated ADMM-IRLS procedure combines the local IRLS update, the global consensus $z$-update, and the local dual update. The whole procedure is summarized in the following algorithm.

Require: local data $\{(X_i,y_i)\}_{i=1}^{m}$; $m$ centers; $\lambda>0$; $\rho>0$; maximum ADMM iterations $K$; maximum IRLS iterations $T$; IRLS tolerance $\epsilon_{\mathrm{IRLS}}$.

Initialize $z^0$ and $u_i^0=0$ for $i=1,\ldots,m$.

for $k=0,1,\ldots,K-1$ do

for all centers $i=1,\ldots,m$ in parallel do

Local update: Each center updates $\beta_i^{k+1}$ locally and in parallel by an IRLS-based local minimization,

$$ \beta_i^{k+1}\approx\arg\min_{\beta_i} \left\{\mathcal{L}_i(\beta_i)+\frac{\rho}{2}\lVert\beta_i-z^k+u_i^k\rVert_2^2\right\}. $$

Initialize $\beta_i^{(0)}=z^k$.

for $t=0,1,\ldots,T-1$ do

Computes: $p_i^{(t)}$, $W_i^{(t)}$, $z_{\mathrm{work}}^{(t)}$ and solves

$$ \beta_i^{(t+1)} =\left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}X_i+\rho I_p\right)^{-1} \left(X_i^{\top}W_i^{(t)}z_{\mathrm{work}}^{(t)}+\rho(z^k-u_i^k)\right). $$

If $\lVert\beta_i^{(t+1)}-\beta_i^{(t)}\rVert_{\infty}\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{IRLS}}$, then break.

end for

Set $\beta_i^{k+1}=\beta_i^{(t_{\mathrm{final}})}$. Both $\beta_i^{k+1}$ and $u_i^k$ are sent to the central server.

end for

Global update ($z$-step): Local updates are aggregated at the central server using

$$ z^{k+1}=S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}\!\left( \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}(\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k) \right). $$

for all centers $i=1,\ldots,m$ in parallel do

Dual update: Scaled dual variables are updated locally at each center according to

$$ u_i^{k+1}=u_i^k+\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}. $$

end for

Stopping criteria: The algorithm is stopped if $r^{k+1}\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}}$ and $s^{k+1}\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}}$.

end for

return $z^{\mathrm{final}}$ (final global sparse coefficient vector).
